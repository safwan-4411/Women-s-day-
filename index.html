A Little History for You

Fun facts about International Women's Day 🌍

📅 1908

15,000 women marched through New York City demanding shorter working hours, better pay, and the right to vote. A spark that changed history.

🌐 1911

The very first official International Women's Day was celebrated in Austria, Denmark, Germany and Switzerland — attended by over a million people!

🇺🇳 1975

The United Nations officially adopted March 8 as International Women's Day, giving it global recognition and a permanent home on the world calendar.

💜 Colors

The official colors are purple (dignity & justice), green (hope), and white (purity). These have symbolized women's equality since the early 1900s.

🌸 Mimosa

In Italy, men traditionally give women mimosa flowers on March 8 as a symbol of strength, sensitivity, and women's resilience. Isn't that lovely?

🌍 Global

Today, Women's Day is celebrated in over 100 countries around the world — from rallies and concerts to quiet acts of appreciation, just like this one. 💛
